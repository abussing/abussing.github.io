I am currently a 4th year PhD student at the University of South Carolina, where I work in Dr. Yen-Yi Ho's lab investigating methods for differential co-expression analysis in single-cell RNAseq data. 

Specifically, I am interested in modeling how the correlation between genes changes within a cell depending on covariates such as spatial location, cell type, and methylation level. We have implemented flexible bayesian models as well as fast frequentist models for addressing this research question-- more details can be found under the Portfolio tab above. In future work, I wish to explore how different cell types communicate with each other within a tissue, using ligands and receptors to regulate cellular processes.

Before attending graduate school, I was in the operations management leadership program at General Electric, where I completed rotations as a lead buyer, manufacturing engineer, and shop operations supervisor.
